Frequently Asked Questions

What is WebP format?

WebP is a modern image format developed by Google that provides superior compression compared to JPEG and PNG. WebP images are typically 25-35% smaller than JPEG and significantly smaller than PNG.

Does my browser support WebP encoding?

Most modern browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Opera) support WebP encoding. Safari supports WebP decoding but may not support encoding. The tool automatically detects your browser's support and notifies you.

Does WebP support transparency?

Yes, WebP supports alpha transparency just like PNG, but with much better compression. This makes it ideal for logos and graphics with transparent backgrounds.

Is my image uploaded to a server?

No. All conversion happens entirely in your browser. Your images never leave your device.

Can I convert multiple files at once?

Yes. Select multiple images and they will be converted in batch. You can download them individually or all at once.
