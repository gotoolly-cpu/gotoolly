Why Extract Pages from a PDF?

Large PDF documents often contain more information than you need. Extracting specific pages lets you share only the relevant content.

  • Share Only What Matters: Extract the relevant chapters, sections, or pages from a large document instead of sending the entire file.
  • Create Custom Documents: Assemble a new PDF by pulling specific pages from different sections of a larger document.
  • Reduce File Size: A document with only the pages you need is smaller and faster to share than a 100+ page PDF.
  • Prepare Submissions: Extract specific pages for grant applications, legal filings, or academic submissions that require only certain sections.
  • Combine with Other Pages: Extract pages from one PDF to later merge with pages from another PDF using the PDF merger tool.

Step-by-Step: Extract PDF Pages

Step 1: Upload Your PDF

What to do: Click the upload area or drag and drop your PDF. The tool shows the total page count.

Step 2: Choose Page Selection Mode

What to do: Select All Pages or Specific Pages mode.

Why it matters: Specific Pages mode allows you to enter custom page ranges.

Step 3: Enter Page Numbers

What to do: Enter page ranges like 1-3, 5, 7-9, 12. Use commas to separate ranges and individual pages.

Why it matters: Pages are extracted in the order you specify. The new PDF preserves this order.

Common mistake: Using invalid page numbers that exceed the document's page count. Check the total pages before entering ranges.

Step 4: Extract and Download

What to do: Click Extract. The new PDF is generated with only the selected pages. Download the result.

Why it matters: The extracted PDF maintains all original formatting. Verify the page count matches your selection.

Best Practices

Tip: Keep the original intact. The extraction does not modify your original file. You can extract different page combinations from the same source.

Tip: Double-check page numbers. PDFs often have a cover page or table of contents. Account for these when entering page ranges.

Tip: Use extraction before merging. Extract the pages you need from multiple PDFs, then merge them into one comprehensive document.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Off-by-One Page Numbers

The Problem: You extract pages 3-5 but intended to get pages 4-6.

Fix: Always check the page count and use the thumbnail preview to verify which pages you are selecting.

Mistake 2: Using Invalid Range Format

The Problem: The tool does not recognize the page range format.

Fix: Use the format: 1-5, 8, 11-13. Use hyphens for ranges and commas to separate them.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I extract non-consecutive pages from a PDF?

Yes. You can enter non-consecutive page ranges and individual page numbers, such as 1-3, 5, 7-9, 12. The extractor will create a new PDF containing only those pages in the order you specified.

What happens to the extracted pages formatting?

The extracted pages retain all original formatting, fonts, images, and layout. Each page in the new PDF is identical to how it appeared in the source document.

Can I extract a single page from a PDF?

Yes. Simply enter a single page number (e.g., 5) to extract just that one page as a new PDF document.

What is the difference between extracting pages and splitting a PDF?

Extracting pages lets you pick specific pages to create a new document. Splitting a PDF divides the document into multiple files based on page ranges.

Is my PDF uploaded to any server?

No. The entire extraction process happens locally in your browser. Your PDF never leaves your device.
