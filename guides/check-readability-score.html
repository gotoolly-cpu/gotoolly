Why Check Readability?

Readability measures how easily a reader can understand your writing. Checking it ensures your content reaches your intended audience.

  • SEO Benefits: Search engines rank content higher when it is clear and readable. Lower grade levels tend to perform better.
  • Audience Matching: Ensure your content matches the reading level of your target audience.
  • Professional Communication: Emails, reports, and proposals are more effective when written clearly.
  • Accessibility: Readable content is accessible to more people, including non-native speakers.
  • User Engagement: Clear text keeps readers on your page longer and reduces bounce rates.

Understanding Flesch-Kincaid Scores

The Flesch-Kincaid system produces two scores:

  • Grade Level: The US school grade level needed to understand the text. A score of 8 means an 8th grader can read it.
  • Reading Ease: A score from 0-100. Higher means easier to read. 60-70 is considered plain English.

Both scores use sentence length and syllable count as factors. Shorter sentences and simpler words produce easier scores.

Step-by-Step: Check Your Text Readability

Step 1: Paste Your Text

What to do: Paste your text into the tool's textarea. For accurate results, include at least 100 words.

Why it matters: Longer samples produce more reliable scores. Very short texts may produce misleading results.

Common mistake: Testing only one paragraph. Paste your full article or section for a representative score.

Step 2: Review the Results

What to do: The tool instantly shows the Flesch-Kincaid grade level, reading ease score, and other statistics.

Why it matters: The grade level tells you who can read your content. The reading ease gives you a quick quality indicator.

Common mistake: Focusing on only one metric. Consider both grade level and reading ease for a complete picture.

Step 3: Improve Based on Results

What to do: If your score is too high, break long sentences, replace complex words, and reduce passive voice.

Why it matters: Making targeted improvements based on actual measurements is more effective than guessing.

Common mistake: Trying to achieve the lowest possible score. Some technical terms are necessary; aim for your audience's level.

Best Practices

Tip: Target grade 6-8 for web content. This is the sweet spot for general audiences and SEO. Technical documentation can be higher.

Tip: Break long sentences. If your average sentence length is over 20 words, split them. Shorter sentences are easier to read and score better.

Tip: Use active voice. "The team completed the project" scores better than "The project was completed by the team."

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good readability score?

A Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level of 6-8 is ideal for general web content. This means a 6th to 8th grader can read it. For technical documentation, grade 8-10 is acceptable.

How is the readability score calculated?

The Flesch-Kincaid formula uses average sentence length and average syllables per word. Shorter sentences and simpler words produce lower (easier) grade levels.

Does readability score affect SEO?

Yes. Search engines favor content that is easy to read. Posts with lower readability scores tend to rank better because they satisfy more users.
