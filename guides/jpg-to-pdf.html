Why Convert JPG to PDF?

JPG images are great for photos but awkward for sharing multiple images as a set. PDF brings them together in one organized, printable document.

  • Single File: Combine dozens of JPG images into one PDF instead of sending multiple attachments or a ZIP archive.
  • Print Ready: PDF pages print consistently with proper margins and page sizes, unlike printing individual images.
  • Universal Access: Anyone can open a PDF without needing image editing software or even knowing how to navigate a photo gallery.
  • Preserved Quality: Images in PDF remain high resolution. The conversion does not recompress or degrade your JPG quality.
  • Document Structure: Add page numbers, bookmarks, or security to the resulting PDF for professional presentation.

Understanding Page Settings

Before converting, you can customize several page options to control how your images appear in the PDF.

Setting Options Best For
Page Size A4, Letter, Legal, Original, Custom Matching document standards or preserving image dimensions
Orientation Portrait, Landscape, Auto Auto adjusts per image for mixed aspect ratios
Margins None, Small, Medium, Large None for edge-to-edge images, Medium for documents
Image Fit Contain, Cover, Stretch Contain preserves aspect ratio, Stretch fills the page

Tip: Use Original page size with No margins for scanned documents that need to match the source dimensions exactly.

Step-by-Step: Convert Your JPG Images

Step 1: Upload Your JPG Images

What to do: Click the upload area or drag and drop your JPG files. You can upload multiple images at once.

Why it matters: Batch uploading saves time. All images are loaded into browser memory simultaneously for quick processing.

Common mistake: Uploading non-JPG files. The tool accepts JPG/JPEG images. Convert other formats first if needed.

Step 2: Reorder Your Images

What to do: Drag and drop thumbnails to arrange them in the desired order. The top image becomes page one of the PDF.

Why it matters: The PDF follows the exact order you set. This is critical for scanned documents where page sequence matters.

Common mistake: Forgetting to check the order before converting. Always scan through the thumbnail list to confirm the sequence.

Step 3: Set Page Options

What to do: Choose page size, orientation, margins, and image fit from the settings panel.

Why it matters: These settings control how each image fits on its page. Auto orientation adjusts per image, while fixed orientation applies to all.

Common mistake: Using fixed Portrait orientation for a mix of portrait and landscape photos, causing landscape images to have empty side bars.

Step 4: Convert and Download

What to do: Click Convert to PDF. The tool processes each image and generates a single PDF with one image per page.

Why it matters: The PDF is generated locally and downloaded instantly. Each image becomes a separate page preserving its resolution.

Common mistake: Closing the tab before the download starts. Wait for the conversion progress to complete fully.

Best Practices for Professional Results

Tip: Sort images before uploading. Rename your files in the desired order (e.g., page-01.jpg, page-02.jpg) to make reordering easier.

Tip: Check image resolution. For print PDFs, ensure images are at least 300 DPI at the page size. Low-resolution images will look pixelated in print.

Tip: Use Contain fit for mixed sizes. This ensures all images fit within the page area without cropping while preserving their original aspect ratios.

Tip: Pre-crop images. If your JPG files have uneven borders or unwanted areas, crop them in any image editor before uploading for cleaner results.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Not Checking Image Order

The Problem: The PDF pages are in the wrong sequence, requiring a complete redo.

Why it Fails: Images appear in the order they were selected unless manually rearranged.

Fix: Always arrange thumbnails by dragging them into the correct order before clicking Convert.

Mistake 2: Using Wrong Page Size

The Problem: Images are too small on the page or get cut off at the edges.

Why it Fails: The page size and image fit settings determine how images are placed on each page.

Fix: Match the page size to your images. Use Original size for scans and Standard sizes for documents intended for printing.

Mistake 3: Overlooking Image Quality

The Problem: The PDF looks blurry or pixelated when printed.

Why it Fails: Low-resolution JPG images cannot magically become high quality in PDF format.

Fix: Start with high-resolution source images. Use images at least 1500 pixels wide for good print results.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I reorder JPG images before converting to PDF?

Yes. After uploading your JPG files, you can drag and drop them into any order. The final PDF will follow the sequence you set, making it easy to arrange scanned pages or photo albums.

What page size options are available?

You can choose from standard sizes like A4, Letter, Legal, or match the original image dimensions. You can also set custom width and height values for non-standard layouts.

Can I mix different image orientations in one PDF?

Yes. When you set orientation to Auto, each page adapts to match the image aspect ratio. Portrait images get portrait pages and landscape images get landscape pages within the same PDF.

How many JPG images can I combine at once?

You can combine as many JPG images as your browser memory allows. For practical purposes, 50-100 images work reliably. Very large batches may require more processing time.

Is my JPG file uploaded to any server?

No. The entire conversion happens locally in your browser using JavaScript. Your images never leave your device, ensuring complete privacy for sensitive documents.
