Why Delete Pages from a PDF?

PDFs often accumulate unwanted pages during creation or scanning. Removing them makes your documents cleaner, smaller, and more professional.

  • Remove Blank Pages: Scanned documents and PDF conversions often produce blank pages that should be removed.
  • Delete Cover Pages: Remove unnecessary title pages or covers when sharing just the content.
  • Eliminate Duplicates: Fix issues where pages were accidentally duplicated during scanning or assembly.
  • Reduce File Size: Fewer pages means a smaller file that is faster to email and easier to store.
  • Clean Up Drafts: Remove draft watermarks, notes, or placeholder pages from final versions.

Step-by-Step: Delete PDF Pages

Step 1: Upload Your PDF

What to do: Click the upload area or drag and drop your PDF. Thumbnail previews of all pages are displayed.

Step 2: Select Pages to Delete

What to do: Click on page thumbnails to select them. Selected pages are highlighted. Use Ctrl+Click (Cmd+Click on Mac) to select multiple non-consecutive pages.

Common mistake: Accidentally clicking the wrong page. Check the thumbnail carefully before confirming deletion.

Step 3: Delete Selected Pages

What to do: Click the Delete button. Confirm the deletion if prompted.

Why it matters: The tool removes the selected pages and renumbers the remaining pages automatically.

Step 4: Download the Result

What to do: Once satisfied, download the modified PDF with the pages removed.

Why it matters: The new PDF is generated locally with only the remaining pages.

Best Practices

Tip: Keep the original file. Always save a copy of the original PDF before deleting pages. Mistakes happen and you may need to restore a page later.

Tip: Review the thumbnail preview. Thumbnails give you a visual confirmation of which pages you are deleting. Use them to avoid errors.

Tip: Check page count after deletion. Verify the total page count matches your expectations. If a 10-page document becomes 9, you deleted one page as intended.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Deleting the Wrong Page

The Problem: You realize too late that you deleted the wrong page.

Fix: Always use the thumbnail preview to visually confirm which page you are deleting. Keep a backup of the original file.

Mistake 2: Forgetting to Check for Blank Pages

The Problem: The final document still has blank pages that reduce its professional appearance.

Fix: Scroll through all thumbnails to identify and remove any blank or unwanted pages before downloading.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I delete multiple pages at once?

Yes. You can select multiple pages by holding Ctrl (Cmd on Mac) and clicking thumbnails, or enter a page range like 3-7 to delete a consecutive block of pages.

Can I undo a page deletion?

The tool may offer an undo option within the current session before you download. However, once you download the modified PDF and close the tab, the original is not recoverable through the tool.

What happens to page numbers after deletion?

Pages are automatically renumbered after deletion. For example, if you delete page 5 from a 10-page document, the old page 6 becomes the new page 5.

Can I delete non-consecutive pages in one operation?

Yes. Select multiple non-consecutive pages by holding the Ctrl (Cmd) key and clicking each page thumbnail you want to remove, then click delete.

Is my PDF uploaded to any server?

No. The entire page deletion process happens locally in your browser. Your PDF never leaves your device.
