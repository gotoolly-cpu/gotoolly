Why Add Text to PDF?

PDFs are the standard for sharing finished documents, but they often lock you out of making simple additions. Adding text to a PDF lets you annotate, mark up, and customize without paying for expensive editing software.

  • Edit Locked PDFs: When you receive a PDF with form fields disabled or text layers locked, adding text overlays lets you fill in blanks, add dates, and insert missing information.
  • Add Dates and Stamps: Date-stamp incoming documents, mark approval dates, or add "Received" stamps for record-keeping and auditing purposes.
  • Create Watermarks: Overlay text like "DRAFT" or "CONFIDENTIAL" across your documents to indicate their status (see our dedicated watermark guide for advanced options).
  • Annotate and Review: Add comments, feedback notes, and review marks to PDFs without printing or using complex annotation tools.
  • Add Signatures: Insert a typed signature line or your name below a signature block to formalize agreements digitally.

Step-by-Step: Add Text to Your PDF

Step 1: Upload Your PDF

What to do: Click the upload area or drag and drop your PDF file. The file loads directly into your browser's memory.

Why it matters: The upload is local (no network transfer), so even large PDFs load quickly. Files up to 25MB are supported.

Common mistake: Dragging a non-PDF file (like an image or Word document). Ensure your file has a .pdf extension.

Step 2: Enter Your Text

What to do: Type the text you want to place on the PDF into the text input field. This can be a single word, a sentence, or a short paragraph.

Why it matters: The text you enter will appear exactly as typed on every page of your PDF. Double-check spelling before proceeding.

Common mistake: Leaving the text field empty. The tool requires at least one character of text to add to the PDF.

Step 3: Choose Font Size and Color

What to do: Select a font size from the dropdown (typically 8pt to 72pt) and pick a color using the color picker.

Why it matters: Font size determines how prominent your text appears. Color helps differentiate your text from existing page content.

Common mistake: Choosing a font size that is too large for the page, causing the text to overflow or look unprofessional.

Step 4: Select Position

What to do: Choose where on the page your text appears using the 9-position grid. Options include top-left, top-center, top-right, middle-left, middle-center, middle-right, bottom-left, bottom-center, and bottom-right.

Why it matters: The position grid gives you quick, predictable placement. For example, use bottom-center for page numbers or top-right for "CONFIDENTIAL" stamps.

Common mistake: Choosing a position that overlaps with existing content, such as placing text in the middle of a page that already has dense content.

Step 5: Set X/Y Offset for Fine-Tuning

What to do: Adjust the X (horizontal) and Y (vertical) offset values in pixels to fine-tune the text position from the selected grid location.

Why it matters: The grid position gets you close. Offsets let you nudge the text by precise amounts for perfect alignment.

Common mistake: Using extreme offset values that push the text off the visible page area.

Step 6: Add Text to PDF and Download

What to do: Click the "Add Text to PDF" button. The tool processes your PDF with the text overlay. Once complete, download the modified PDF.

Why it matters: Processing is instant for most documents. The output maintains the original PDF quality with the new text embedded.

Common mistake: Closing the browser tab before the download completes. Wait for the download prompt to appear.

Best Practices for Great Results

Tip: Use appropriate font size. Match the font size to the purpose: 8-10pt for disclaimers, 12-16pt for annotations, 18-24pt for headers and stamps.

Tip: Test on one page first. Before processing a multi-page document, test your text settings on a single-page PDF to verify positioning and appearance.

Tip: Use the position grid wisely. Choose positions that fit naturally with your document. For example, add dates to the top-right corner where dates traditionally appear.

Tip: Use offsets for fine-tuning. The grid gets you in the ballpark. Use X/Y offsets to align your text precisely with existing page elements.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Text Too Large

The Problem: The added text overflows the page boundaries or covers important content.

Why it Fails: Large font sizes (over 36pt) can exceed the page width, especially for longer text strings.

Fix: Use smaller font sizes for longer text. For short stamps like "CONFIDENTIAL", 18-24pt is usually sufficient.

Mistake 2: Wrong Position Selection

The Problem: The text appears in an awkward or hidden location on the page.

Why it Fails: Some positions (like middle-center) overlay the main content, making the text hard to read.

Fix: Use edge positions (top/bottom) for stamps and annotations. Reserve center positions for watermarks that can overlay content.

Mistake 3: No Text Entered

The Problem: Clicking "Add Text to PDF" without entering any text produces no visible change.

Why it Fails: The tool requires text content to overlay. An empty text field means nothing to add.

Fix: Always type your intended text before clicking the action button. The tool will typically show an error if the field is empty.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I add text to a PDF for free?

Upload your PDF to the GoToolly Add Text to PDF tool, enter your desired text, choose font size, color, and position, then click Add Text to PDF. The tool runs entirely in your browser with no uploads to servers. Download the modified PDF instantly.

Can I choose where the text appears on the page?

Yes. The tool provides a 9-position grid (top-left, top-center, top-right, middle-left, middle-center, middle-right, bottom-left, bottom-center, bottom-right) plus X/Y offset controls for fine-tuning the exact placement of your text.

What font sizes work best?

For most use cases, 12-16pt is ideal for body text annotations, 18-24pt works well for headers and stamps, and 8-10pt is suitable for footnotes and disclaimers. Choose a size that is readable but does not overwhelm the page content.

Can I add text to only some pages?

Currently the tool adds text to every page of your PDF. If you need text on specific pages only, consider splitting your PDF first using the PDF Splitter tool, adding text to the relevant pages, then merging them back together.

Is my PDF uploaded to any server?

No. The entire process happens locally in your browser using JavaScript. Your PDF never leaves your device, ensuring complete privacy for sensitive documents.
