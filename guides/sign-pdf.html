Why Sign PDFs Online?

Contracts, agreements, and official documents often require signatures. Signing digitally is faster, more convenient, and more professional than printing, signing, and scanning.

  • Save Time: Sign documents in seconds without printing, signing by hand, scanning, and emailing.
  • Professional Appearance: Digital signatures look clean and are properly aligned on signature lines.
  • Multiple Signature Methods: Choose the method that works best for you: draw, type, or upload.
  • Work from Anywhere: Sign documents on any device with a browser, no special software required.
  • Privacy: Your signature and document never leave your device.

Your Privacy is Guaranteed

Local Processing: The entire signing process happens in your browser using JavaScript. Your PDF and signature never touch any server.

No Data Collection: We cannot access, view, or store your documents or signature images.

Temporary Memory Only: Your signature exists only in browser memory while the tab is open.

Step-by-Step: Sign Your PDF

Step 1: Upload Your PDF

What to do: Click the upload area or drag and drop your PDF. The document is rendered in your browser.

Step 2: Create Your Signature

What to do: Choose your preferred method: Draw (sign with your input device), Type (enter your name), or Upload (select an image file).

Why it matters: Draw creates a natural signature. Type is quickest. Upload gives you a polished, pre-made signature.

Common mistake: Drawing too small or too large. The signature can be resized after placement.

Step 3: Position Your Signature

What to do: Drag the signature to the desired location on the document. Resize it by dragging the corners.

Why it matters: Precise positioning ensures your signature aligns with signature lines or designated fields.

Common mistake: Placing the signature over existing text. Position it on the signature line or in the designated area.

Step 4: Add Additional Signatures (Optional)

What to do: Create additional signatures for other signatories or multiple locations in the document.

Step 5: Download the Signed PDF

What to do: Click Download to save the PDF with your signature(s) embedded.

Why it matters: The signed PDF is ready to submit, email, or archive.

Best Practices

Tip: Use a touchscreen for drawing. If available, a touchscreen or stylus produces a more natural-looking drawn signature than a mouse.

Tip: Upload a transparent PNG. For the upload method, use a PNG image with a transparent background for the best appearance on the document.

Tip: Check the date. Many signature lines include a date field. Make sure to add the current date alongside your signature.

Tip: Save a clean copy too. Keep both the signed and unsigned versions of important documents for your records.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Signature Too Small or Too Large

The Problem: The signature does not fit properly in the signature box or looks disproportionate.

Fix: Use the resize handles after placing the signature to adjust its size. Most signatures should match the size of a handwritten signature.

Mistake 2: Placing Signature Over Important Content

The Problem: The signature covers text, dates, or other important information on the document.

Fix: Position signatures on designated signature lines or in blank areas. Use the undo feature to replace a misplaced signature.

Frequently Asked Questions

What methods are available for creating a signature?

Three signature methods are available: Draw (use your mouse, touchpad, or touchscreen to sign naturally), Type (type your name and choose a cursive-style font), and Upload (upload an image of your handwritten signature).

Can I position the signature exactly where I want?

Yes. After creating your signature, you can drag it to any position on the page and resize it. This allows precise placement on signature lines or agreement fields.

Is the signature legally binding?

Electronic signatures are legally recognized in most jurisdictions under laws like the ESIGN Act (US) and eIDAS (EU). However, this tool provides a visual signature image, not a digital certificate-based signature.

Can I add multiple signatures to the same document?

Yes. You can add signatures in multiple places on the same document, and multiple different signatures on different signature lines.

Is my PDF uploaded to any server?

No. The entire signing process happens locally in your browser using JavaScript. Your PDF and signature never leave your device.
