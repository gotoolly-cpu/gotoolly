Why Convert PDF to PNG?

PNG is the preferred format when image quality matters most. Unlike JPG, PNG uses lossless compression, making it ideal for text, diagrams, and graphics extracted from PDFs.

  • Lossless Quality: Every pixel of your PDF page is preserved exactly. No compression artifacts, no blurry text, no color degradation.
  • Sharp Text Rendering: PNG handles text and sharp edges perfectly, making it the best choice for document pages with lots of text.
  • Transparency Support: If your PDF contains transparent elements, PNG preserves them while JPG does not support transparency.
  • Professional Publishing: Use PNG images in design software, publication layouts, and high-quality print materials.
  • Archival Quality: For long-term document archiving as images, PNG ensures no generational quality loss.

Best Practices for Professional Results

Tip: Use 300 DPI for documents with small text. Footnotes, fine print, and detailed diagrams need higher resolution to remain legible.

Tip: Watch your file sizes. A 300 DPI, 2x scale PNG of a full-page document can exceed 50 MB. Adjust settings based on your storage and usage needs.

Tip: Use individual downloads for selective pages. If you only need one or two pages, download them individually instead of ZIP to save time.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Using PNG When JPG Would Suffice

The Problem: Extremely large file sizes for photo-heavy PDFs that do not need lossless quality.

Why it Fails: PNG files are significantly larger than JPG for photographic content.

Fix: Use JPG for photo-heavy PDFs and PNG for text, diagrams, and documents where quality matters most.

Mistake 2: Excessive DPI for Screen Use

The Problem: Unnecessarily large files that take longer to download and open.

Why it Fails: Most screens display at 72-150 DPI. 300 DPI images are overkill for screen viewing.

Fix: Use 150 DPI for screen viewing. Reserve 300 DPI for print applications.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between PDF to PNG and PDF to JPG?

PNG uses lossless compression, preserving every detail of the original PDF page with no quality loss. This makes it ideal for text, diagrams, and graphics. JPG uses lossy compression, which reduces file size but can introduce artifacts around text and sharp edges. PNG files are larger but offer superior quality.

What DPI settings are available?

You can choose from 72 DPI (screen quality), 150 DPI (medium quality), 300 DPI (high/print quality), or set a custom DPI value. Higher DPI produces larger, more detailed images.

Can I scale the output images?

Yes. You can set a scale factor from 0.5x to 4x. A 2x scale doubles the pixel dimensions of the output image, useful for creating high-resolution versions of PDF pages.

Can I extract specific pages from my PDF?

Yes. Choose All Pages to convert the entire document, or enter a specific page range such as 1-5, 8, 11-13 to extract only the pages you need.

Is my PDF uploaded to any server?

No. The entire conversion happens locally in your browser using JavaScript. Your PDF never leaves your device, ensuring complete privacy for sensitive documents.
